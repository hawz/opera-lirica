---
title: Salome
compositore: Strauss
anno: "Dresden, 1905"
tagline: "A princess who wants a prophet's head — and gets it"
aria:
  nome: "The Dance of the Seven Veils"
  descrizione: "Salome dances for her stepfather Herod in exchange for a wish. It is one of the most charged scenes in all opera — erotic tension and violence intertwined. Strauss wrote it for an orchestra of one hundred and three players."
personaggi:
  - nome: Salome
    voce: "Dramatic soprano — the protagonist"
    descrizione: "Princess of Judaea, Herodias's daughter. She is sixteen. She is fascinated by the prophet Jochanaan with an obsession that does not distinguish between sacred and profane desire. She is one of the most unsettling characters in modern opera."
  - nome: Jochanaan (John the Baptist)
    voce: "Baritone"
    descrizione: "The prophet imprisoned in the palace cistern. He rejects Salome with horror. His voice rises from the darkness of the cistern like a voice from another world."
  - nome: Herod
    voce: "Character tenor"
    descrizione: "Tetrarch of Judaea, Salome's stepfather. Obsessed with her, prey to superstitions and fears. He grants the prophet's head knowing it is a mistake."
  - nome: Herodias
    voce: "Mezzo-soprano"
    descrizione: "Salome's mother, Herod's wife. She hates the prophet who has condemned her publicly. She wants his death — and uses her daughter as a pawn."
  - nome: Narraboth
    voce: "Tenor"
    descrizione: "Young captain of the guard, in love with Salome. He kills himself when he sees her seeking contact with the prophet. He dies within the opera's first few minutes."
atti:
  - titolo: "Single Scene — A Night at the Palace"
    testo: "Salome is the only opera in a single act — it runs about an hour and forty minutes without interruption. A night at Herod's palace. Salome hears the voice of Jochanaan rising from the cistern where he is imprisoned. She orders him brought out. She sees him. She is immediately obsessed: she wants to touch his hair, his skin, she wants to kiss his mouth. The prophet rejects her with horror and is put back in the cistern."
  - titolo: "The Dance and the Price"
    testo: "Herod asks Salome to dance for him. She refuses. He promises her anything she wants — half his kingdom, jewels, white peacocks. Salome agrees: she wants the head of Jochanaan on a silver platter. Herod is appalled, tries to persuade her to ask for something else. She does not change her mind. Jochanaan is beheaded in the cistern."
  - titolo: "The Final Monologue"
    testo: "The executioner brings Jochanaan's head on a silver platter. Salome takes it. She speaks to it. She tells it he was beautiful. That his voice was the most beautiful sound in the world. Then she kisses it on the mouth. Herod, horrified, orders the soldiers to kill her. They crush her beneath their shields."
finale: "Salome scandalised all of Europe at its premiere: the Kaiser banned it in Berlin, the Metropolitan in New York removed it after a single performance. Strauss said the royalties built him his villa at Garmisch. Today it is considered an absolute masterpiece."
