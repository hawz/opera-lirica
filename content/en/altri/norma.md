---
title: Norma
compositore: Bellini
anno: "Milan, 1831"
tagline: "The great Celtic high priestess betrayed by the man she loves — and by her dearest friend"
aria:
  nome: "Casta diva"
  descrizione: "Norma prays to the moon. It is considered the greatest aria in bel canto — and perhaps one of the most beautiful melodies ever written in the history of Western music."
personaggi:
  - nome: Norma
    voce: "Soprano — the protagonist"
    descrizione: "High druidic priestess in Roman Gaul. She has betrayed her religious vows and her people to love a Roman. She has two secret children with him."
  - nome: Pollione
    voce: "Tenor"
    descrizione: "Roman proconsul, Norma's former lover. He has abandoned her for Adalgisa. He is not particularly sympathetic."
  - nome: Adalgisa
    voce: "Mezzo-soprano"
    descrizione: "A young priestess, Norma's friend. She has fallen in love with Pollione without knowing of his bond with Norma. When she discovers the truth, she is devastated."
atti:
  - titolo: "Act I — The Secret Revealed"
    testo: "The Gaulish druids hate the Romans and want war. Norma restrains them — secretly because she loves Pollione. Adalgisa confesses to her that she loves a Roman. Norma understands her, almost forgives her — until she discovers that the Roman is Pollione, the father of her own children. The duet between the two women is one of the most beautiful moments in all bel canto history."
  - titolo: "Act II — The Final Choice"
    testo: "Norma considers killing her children — she cannot do it. She considers entrusting them to Adalgisa. Then Pollione is captured. Norma could save him if Adalgisa gives him up. Adalgisa refuses. Norma is transformed: she reveals to the druids that among them is a priestess who has violated her vows. 'Who is it?' 'It is I.' She surrenders herself to the pyre. Pollione, struck by her greatness, asks to die with her. They climb onto the pyre together."
finale: "Norma chooses sacrifice not out of despair but out of dignity: she will not betray herself, and will not let her children grow up with a dishonoured mother. She mounts the pyre with her head held high. It is the noblest death in opera."
