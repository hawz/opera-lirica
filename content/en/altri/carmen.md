---
title: Carmen
compositore: Bizet
anno: "Paris, 1875"
tagline: "The freest woman in opera — and the man who kills her because he cannot possess her"
aria:
  nome: "L'amour est un oiseau rebelle — The Habanera"
  descrizione: "Carmen describes love as a wild bird that cannot be caught. It is her declaration of freedom. It is impossible not to recognise it."
personaggi:
  - nome: Carmen
    voce: "Mezzo-soprano"
    descrizione: "Gypsy cigarette-maker, free and wild. She loves whom she wants, when she wants. She would rather die than surrender her freedom."
  - nome: Don José
    voce: "Tenor"
    descrizione: "An honest soldier, engaged to a good country girl. Carmen destroys him: he gives up everything for her, becomes a brigand. When she abandons him, he kills her."
  - nome: Escamillo
    voce: "Baritone — the toreador"
    descrizione: "Famous bullfighter, self-assured. Carmen's new love. He sings the Toreador Song with the same ease with which he lives."
  - nome: Micaëla
    voce: "Soprano"
    descrizione: "Don José's good fiancée. The woman he should have married. She represents everything he betrays."
atti:
  - titolo: "Acts I and II — The Seduction"
    testo: "Seville, outside the cigarette factory. Carmen singles out Don José from all the soldiers and throws him a flower. He is already lost. When Carmen is arrested for a brawl, José lets her escape — and ends up in jail. He gets out, runs to her. She dances for him in the tavern. He must return to barracks. She taunts him: he does not truly love her if he leaves. José stays. He has deserted. There is no going back."
  - titolo: "Acts III and IV — The Abandonment and the End"
    testo: "Among the smugglers in the mountains, Carmen reads the cards. Death — first for her, then for him. She does not care: she is not afraid. Then Escamillo arrives, the bullfighter. Carmen falls in love with him. Don José understands it is over. News reaches him that his mother is dying — he goes. When he returns, Carmen has already fallen for another. Outside the bullring, José begs her one last time. Carmen throws to the ground the ring he had given her. He stabs her."
finale: "Carmen is no victim: she chooses freedom until the very last second, knowing what it might cost her. Don José is no murderer in the ordinary sense: he is a man who has lost everything — and does not know how to live without her. Bizet takes no one's side. And he is right."
