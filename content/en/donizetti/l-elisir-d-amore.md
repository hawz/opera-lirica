---
title: L'elisir d'amore
compositore: Donizetti
anno: "Milan, 1832"
tagline: "A naïve boy, a charlatan, a potion that is just wine — and a love that works anyway"
aria:
  nome: "Una furtiva lagrima"
  descrizione: "Nemorino sees a tear on Adina's cheek and understands she loves him. It is one of the purest and most moving tenor arias in the repertoire — written, according to legend, in a single night."
personaggi:
  - nome: Nemorino
    voce: "Tenor — the protagonist"
    descrizione: "A simple peasant, a little naïve, hopelessly in love with Adina. His disarming goodness is the heart of the opera."
  - nome: Adina
    voce: "Soprano"
    descrizione: "Beautiful, educated, independent. She reads Orlando Furioso to the village women. She cares for Nemorino but will not admit it until the very last moment."
  - nome: Dulcamara
    voce: "Bass buffo — the charlatan"
    descrizione: "A travelling salesman of miracle cures. He sells Nemorino a 'love potion' that is simply Bordeaux. The most likeable comic character in the repertoire."
  - nome: Belcore
    voce: "Baritone"
    descrizione: "An army sergeant, self-assured, courting Adina. Nemorino's rival — vain but not wicked."
atti:
  - titolo: "Act I — The Elixir"
    testo: "A village in the Basque country. Nemorino has always loved Adina but does not dare declare himself. Adina reads aloud the story of Tristan and Isolde and the love potion. The charlatan Dulcamara arrives with his cart full of cures for everything. Nemorino asks for the love elixir. Dulcamara sells him a bottle of red wine, telling him it will work within twenty-four hours — but he will already be gone. Nemorino drinks and immediately feels invincible. His breezy new behaviour bewilders and irritates Adina, who out of spite agrees to marry the sergeant Belcore."
  - titolo: "Act II — The Tear"
    testo: "Nemorino, desperate, enlists in the army to get money for more potion. Meanwhile news spreads that his rich uncle has died leaving him his inheritance — news he himself does not yet know. All the village girls begin to court him. Nemorino does not understand: it must be the elixir working! Adina watches him, realises she truly loves him, buys out his army enlistment. Nemorino sees her shed a tear. He has his answer."
finale: "L'elisir d'amore proves that Donizetti could be light without being shallow. Beneath the comedy is a real story: a man who loves without conditions, and a woman who learns to admit it. The happy ending is earned, not given."
