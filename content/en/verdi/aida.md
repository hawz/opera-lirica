---
title: Aida
compositore: Verdi
anno: "Cairo, 1871"
tagline: "Egypt, pyramids, war and two lovers buried alive together"
aria:
  nome: "Celeste Aida"
  descrizione: "Radamès sings his dream of love before war sweeps everything away. One of the most celebrated tenor arias in history."
personaggi:
  - nome: Aida
    voce: "Soprano"
    descrizione: "Ethiopian slave at the Pharaoh's court. In truth she is the daughter of the Ethiopian king. She loves Radamès, the Egyptian general — her people's enemy."
  - nome: Radamès
    voce: "Tenor"
    descrizione: "Egyptian general, war hero, in love with Aida. He betrays his homeland for her and is condemned to death."
  - nome: Amneris
    voce: "Mezzo-soprano"
    descrizione: "The Pharaoh's daughter, she loves Radamès with a desperate, obsessive love. She knows he loves Aida. She is the most tormented character."
  - nome: Amonasro
    voce: "Baritone"
    descrizione: "King of Ethiopia and Aida's father. A prisoner of the Egyptians, he uses his daughter as a political pawn."
atti:
  - titolo: "Acts I and II — The War and the Triumph"
    testo: "Egypt is at war with Ethiopia. Radamès is chosen to command the army. Aida is torn: he will go to fight against her father and her people. Radamès wins the war and returns in triumph. The Pharaoh grants him any wish. Among the prisoners is Amonasro, Aida's father — who hides his identity as king."
  - titolo: "Act III — The Forced Betrayal"
    testo: "At night, by the Nile. Amonasro persuades Aida to extract from Radamès the secret of the Egyptian army's route — information that would allow the Ethiopians to ambush them. Aida, torn apart, does it. Radamès reveals the military plans. Amonasro exults — he had been listening. Radamès understands he has been used. He is arrested as a traitor."
  - titolo: "Act IV — The Tomb"
    testo: "Amneris begs Radamès to defend himself, to save himself. All he has to do is give up Aida. He refuses. He is condemned to be walled alive in a tomb beneath the temple. When the stone falls, Radamès hears a breath: Aida has hidden inside to die with him. They embrace in the darkness while above them Amneris weeps."
finale: "Two lovers die embracing in the darkness while the temple resounds with prayers. Above them, Amneris implores peace for them. It is among the most beautiful and heartbreaking finales in all opera history."
