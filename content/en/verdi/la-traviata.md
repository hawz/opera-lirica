---
title: La traviata
compositore: Verdi
anno: "Venice, 1853"
tagline: "A courtesan who sacrifices herself for love. Paris, champagne, consumption and broken hearts."
aria:
  nome: "Addio del passato"
  descrizione: "Violetta, alone and ill, bids farewell to happier days. A melody of heartbreaking simplicity."
personaggi:
  - nome: Violetta Valéry
    voce: "Soprano — the protagonist"
    descrizione: "Parisian courtesan, brilliant, dying of consumption. She finally allows herself to love — and is punished by society for it."
  - nome: Alfredo Germont
    voce: "Tenor — the lover"
    descrizione: "A passionate young bourgeois. He loves her with everything he has, but lacks the courage to defend her enough."
  - nome: Giorgio Germont
    voce: "Baritone — the father"
    descrizione: "Alfredo's father. He is not a villain: he is a man of his time, convinced he is doing the right thing. He asks Violetta to disappear from his son's life. And she agrees."
atti:
  - titolo: "Act I — The Ball, the Toast, the Love"
    testo: "Paris, 1850. Violetta's house, a glittering party. Alfredo, a young man from the provinces, confesses he has loved her for a year. Violetta is amused: she does not believe in love. But that night, after the guests leave, for the first time she feels something real. She sings her inner battle: freedom or love? She chooses love."
  - titolo: "Act II — Happiness Shattered"
    testo: "They live together in the country; Violetta has sold everything to support them. Alfredo's father arrives. He does not shout or threaten — it is worse: he calmly explains that Alfredo's sister cannot marry if he stays with a courtesan. He asks her, with all the gentleness in the world, to disappear. Violetta writes Alfredo a farewell letter and leaves. Alfredo, furious, follows her to Paris and humiliates her publicly, throwing money at her: payment for her services. It is the opera's cruellest scene."
  - titolo: "Act III — The Return, Too Late"
    testo: "Violetta is dying, alone, penniless. Alfredo's father has written to tell her his son knows the truth and is on his way. Alfredo enters, holds her, begs forgiveness, promises a life together far from Paris. Violetta smiles, rises — and collapses. She dies in his arms."
finale: "La traviata is the story of a woman destroyed not by sin but by mercy — the mercy with which she chooses to sacrifice herself for the happiness of those she loves. Verdi loved it more than any other of his operas."
