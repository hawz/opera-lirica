---
title: Madama Butterfly
compositore: Puccini
anno: "Milan, 1904"
tagline: "A geisha who waits. Three years. With a child. In vain."
aria:
  nome: "Un bel dì vedremo"
  descrizione: "Butterfly imagines Pinkerton's return: the ship in the harbour, him climbing the hill. It is the most heartbreaking of Puccini's arias because we know it will never happen."
personaggi:
  - nome: Cio-Cio-San (Butterfly)
    voce: "Soprano"
    descrizione: "Fifteen-year-old Japanese geisha. She converts to Christianity to marry Pinkerton. She waits three years for his return. She is the symbol of betrayed love."
  - nome: Pinkerton
    voce: "Tenor"
    descrizione: "American naval officer. He marries Butterfly 'for the duration of his stay in Japan' — a formula legally valid in Japan but morally hollow. He returns with an American wife."
  - nome: Suzuki
    voce: "Mezzo-soprano"
    descrizione: "Butterfly's faithful maid. She knows the truth before Butterfly does, and her loyalty is heartbreaking."
  - nome: Sharpless
    voce: "Baritone"
    descrizione: "American consul. He tries to warn Butterfly — but she will not listen. He is the voice of reality that no one heeds."
atti:
  - titolo: "Act I — The Wedding"
    testo: "Nagasaki, 1900. Pinkerton marries Butterfly lightly, almost as an exotic game. The consul Sharpless tries to caution him: she is serious, she truly believes in this. Pinkerton laughs. That night, in the moonlight, the two sing of their love. It is the opera's most beautiful scene — and the most poisoned, because we already know how it will end."
  - titolo: "Act II — The Wait"
    testo: "Three years later. Pinkerton is in America. Butterfly has a child — his son. Suzuki no longer believes he will return. Butterfly is unshakeable: he will come back. Sharpless brings a letter from Pinkerton, tries to read it — Butterfly will not let him finish, she is too happy just hearing his name. Then an American ship appears in the harbour. Butterfly, Suzuki and the child wait all night at the window."
  - titolo: "Act III — The Truth"
    testo: "At dawn, Pinkerton has arrived — but he is not alone. With him is his American wife, Kate. Suzuki must tell Butterfly the truth. They want the child, to take him to America. Butterfly understands everything in an instant. She asks them to return in half an hour. She holds the child, lets him play with a doll and an American flag, blindfolds him. Then she takes the dagger with which her father had taken his own life. She kills herself. Pinkerton bursts in calling her name. It is too late."
finale: "Butterfly dies with no one able to save her — not for lack of will, but because her love was too great for the world around her. Puccini wept every time he conducted this opera."
