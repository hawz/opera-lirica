---
title: Tosca
compositore: Puccini
anno: "Rome, 1900"
tagline: "A political thriller in three acts — a traitor, a diva and a leap from the tower"
aria:
  nome: "Vissi d'arte"
  descrizione: "Tosca asks God why he is punishing her, she who has always lived for art and love. A desperate prayer in the middle of a nightmare."
personaggi:
  - nome: Floria Tosca
    voce: "Soprano — the protagonist"
    descrizione: "Famous opera singer, passionate, intensely jealous. She loves Cavaradossi with absolute intensity. It is she who holds everyone's fate in her hands."
  - nome: Mario Cavaradossi
    voce: "Tenor"
    descrizione: "Painter, Tosca's lover, liberal sympathies. He helps a political fugitive — and it leads him to his death."
  - nome: Baron Scarpia
    voce: "Baritone — the villain"
    descrizione: "Chief of the papal police. Cruel, refined, hypocritical. He uses power to satisfy his desires. He desires Tosca. He is one of the most hated and fascinating villains in opera history."
atti:
  - titolo: "Act I — Rome, 1800. The Church"
    testo: "A political prisoner escapes from jail and takes refuge in the church where Cavaradossi is painting. Cavaradossi helps him. Tosca arrives — beautiful, jealous as ever. Then Scarpia arrives, looking for the fugitive. He uses Tosca's jealousy as a lever to track him down."
  - titolo: "Act II — The Police Palace"
    testo: "Scarpia has Cavaradossi arrested and tortured. He summons Tosca and offers her a deal: if she becomes his lover, Cavaradossi will be shot with blank cartridges — a mock execution, then both go free. Tosca accepts. She signs the safe-conduct. Scarpia moves to embrace her. Tosca picks up a knife from the table and stabs him. 'This is Tosca's kiss!' — then places two candles beside the body and leaves."
  - titolo: "Act III — Castel Sant'Angelo, at Dawn"
    testo: "Tosca reaches Cavaradossi on the roof of the castle. She explains the plan: the execution is fake, he must fall like an actor. The firing squad shoots. Cavaradossi falls. Tosca runs to him laughing. She shakes him. He does not get up. Scarpia betrayed her: the bullets were real. At that moment the soldiers arrive. Tosca climbs onto the parapet. 'O Scarpia, before God!' — and leaps into the void."
finale: "Tosca is the opera where nobody wins: the villain dies, the good man dies, and the protagonist takes her own life. Yet it is not nihilistic — it is the story of a woman who chooses to die free rather than live defeated."
