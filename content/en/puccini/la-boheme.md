---
title: La Bohème
compositore: Puccini
anno: "Turin, 1896"
tagline: "Youth, poverty, love and a death you never forget"
aria:
  nome: "Che gelida manina / Sì, mi chiamano Mimì"
  descrizione: "The double portrait of the protagonists at their first meeting. Two arias that feel like the opening chapter of a story you already know the ending of."
personaggi:
  - nome: Rodolfo
    voce: "Tenor — the poet"
    descrizione: "Young bohemian poet, romantic, a little selfish. He loves Mimì with everything he has but cannot stay by her side."
  - nome: Mimì
    voce: "Soprano"
    descrizione: "Embroiderer, shy, dying of consumption. She falls in love with Rodolfo and dies loving him. She is the quintessential Puccini heroine."
  - nome: Musetta
    voce: "Soprano — the vivacious friend"
    descrizione: "Beautiful, capricious, she loves and leaves. A cheerful counterpoint to Mimì's tragic story."
  - nome: Marcello
    voce: "Baritone — the painter"
    descrizione: "Rodolfo's best friend. He loves Musetta stormily. Companion of a beautiful, penniless youth."
atti:
  - titolo: "Act I — The Candle and the First Meeting"
    testo: "Paris, Christmas Eve, a freezing attic. Rodolfo and his bohemian friends burn the poet's manuscripts to keep warm. A knock at the door: it is Mimì, the neighbour, asking to light her candle. The candle goes out again — perhaps not by accident. In the darkness their hands meet. 'Che gelida manina' sings Rodolfo. They fall in love within five minutes."
  - titolo: "Acts II and III — The Latin Quarter and the Break-up"
    testo: "Christmas night at Café Momus: merriment, wine, Musetta driving Marcello wild. But winter deepens. Rodolfo and Mimì quarrel, separate. Rodolfo confesses the truth to Marcello: he left Mimì because she is ill and he is too poor to care for her. Poverty destroys love — not jealousy, not betrayal."
  - titolo: "Act IV — The Return, the End"
    testo: "Months later, in the same attic. Rodolfo and Marcello pretend cheerfulness but think of their lost loves. Musetta bursts in: Mimì is outside, she can no longer climb the stairs. They bring her in. Mimì is a shadow of herself. But she is at peace — she has returned to where she was happy. The friends go out to find medicine and a muff to warm her hands. They are left alone. Mimì falls asleep. Rodolfo watches her smile, thinks she is better. Marcello returns. Musetta weeps. Rodolfo understands everything."
finale: "La Bohème has no finale in the traditional sense: Mimì dies and the opera ends. Full stop. Puccini knew that adding anything would be wrong. The silence after the last note is part of the opera."
