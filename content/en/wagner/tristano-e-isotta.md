---
title: Tristan and Isolde
compositore: Wagner
anno: "Munich, 1865"
tagline: "The opera that changed the history of music — an impossible love that lasts all night"
aria:
  nome: "Liebestod — Love Death"
  descrizione: "Isolde sings over Tristan's dead body and dissolves into love itself. It is the resolution of a harmony left unresolved for three hours. When it finally arrives, it feels as though the whole world exhales."
personaggi:
  - nome: Tristan
    voce: "Dramatic tenor"
    descrizione: "Irish knight in the service of King Mark of Cornwall. Noble, loyal, fated. He drinks the potion and there is no return."
  - nome: Isolde
    voce: "Dramatic soprano"
    descrizione: "Irish princess betrothed to King Mark. She hates Tristan — then drinks the potion. Her final Liebestod is among the most extraordinary moments in music history."
  - nome: King Mark
    voce: "Bass"
    descrizione: "King of Cornwall, Tristan's uncle. He is no villain: he is a man betrayed who cannot bring himself to hate those he loves. His monologue in Act Two is devastating."
  - nome: Brangäne
    voce: "Mezzo-soprano"
    descrizione: "Isolde's handmaid. She accidentally administers the love potion instead of the poison. She carries the weight of this for the entire opera."
  - nome: Kurwenal
    voce: "Baritone"
    descrizione: "Tristan's faithful squire. He follows him to death with absolute, silent loyalty."
atti:
  - titolo: "Act I — The Potion"
    testo: "A ship at sea. Tristan is escorting Isolde to Cornwall to marry King Mark. Isolde hates him: he killed her fiancé Morold, then came to ask for her hand for his king. She orders Brangäne to prepare a poison: she wants to die together with Tristan. Brangäne substitutes the poison with the love potion. They both drink. They look at each other. There is nothing to be done."
  - titolo: "Act II — The Night"
    testo: "King Mark has left for a hunting party. Tristan and Isolde meet at night in the castle garden. They sing of their love in an immense duet — the night as the only space in which to exist, the day as the enemy that separates them. Then the traitor Melot arrives with King Mark. Mark is shattered: the nephew he loved as a son, the promised bride. He does not shout. He only asks why. Tristan cannot answer. He lets himself be wounded by Melot."
  - titolo: "Act III — The Wait and the Death"
    testo: "Tristan's castle in Brittany. Tristan is wounded, feverish, delirious. He waits for Isolde — only she can heal him. Kurwenal has sent for her. Tristan alternates between states of ecstasy and despair. Finally the ship arrives. Tristan tears off his bandages and runs towards her. He dies in her arms before he can embrace her. King Mark also arrives — he came to forgive them. It is too late. Isolde sings her Liebestod and dies."
finale: "Tristan and Isolde has no plot in the traditional sense: it is an experience. Wagner constructed over three hours a never-resolved harmonic tension — a famous chord, the 'Tristan chord', that never resolves until the opera's final minute. It is the music of desire: always waiting, never satisfied. Until the end."
