---
title: The Marriage of Figaro
compositore: Mozart
anno: "Vienna, 1786"
tagline: "A frantic comedy about love, power and the art of unmasking the powerful"
aria:
  nome: "Porgi, amor"
  descrizione: "The Countess begs Love to return her husband to her — or let her die. One of the most beautiful melodies ever written."
personaggi:
  - nome: Figaro
    voce: "Baritone — the protagonist"
    descrizione: "The Count's servant, brilliant, cunning and in love. He is the true hero of the story."
  - nome: Susanna
    voce: "Soprano — Figaro's fiancée"
    descrizione: "The Countess's maid. Just as clever as Figaro, and often the one who saves the day."
  - nome: Count Almaviva
    voce: "Baritone — the villain"
    descrizione: "A powerful, selfish, libertine nobleman. He holds the power of life and death over his servants — and uses it."
  - nome: The Countess
    voce: "Soprano — the Count's wife"
    descrizione: "Noble, dignified, deeply wounded by her husband's indifference. Her musical moments are the most beautiful in the opera."
  - nome: Cherubino
    voce: "Mezzo-soprano — the page"
    descrizione: "A teenage boy madly in love with every woman in the castle. Comic and tender at once."
atti:
  - titolo: "Act I — The Eve of the Wedding"
    testo: "Figaro and Susanna are about to be married. The problem is that the Count has set his eyes on Susanna and wants to exercise the right of the first night — a medieval privilege allowing the master to take the bride before her husband. Figaro discovers the plan and swears to foil it. Meanwhile, Cherubino — the teenage page — is hopelessly in love with every woman in the castle and creates chaos wherever he goes."
  - titolo: "Acts II and III — Disguises and Deceptions"
    testo: "Susanna and the Countess join forces against the Count. The plan: make the Count believe he has a secret rendezvous with Susanna, and send the Countess in disguise instead. In the middle: endless misunderstandings, people hiding behind chairs and windows, a page jumping out of a window, and the Count constantly made to look foolish without understanding why. The scene where the Countess forgives Susanna — and then asks forgiveness herself — is one of the most moving in all opera history."
  - titolo: "Act IV — The Triumph of the Servants"
    testo: "In the garden at night, the chaos reaches its peak. The Count woos who he believes to be Susanna — but it is his own wife. He is unmasked, humiliated before everyone. The Countess, with extraordinary dignity, forgives him. The Count, for the first time, genuinely kneels and apologises."
finale: "Everyone is reconciled, the wedding is celebrated, and Mozart leaves us with the feeling that love — even when it hurts — is always worth it. It is an opera that laughs at power and celebrates the intelligence of those who have none."
