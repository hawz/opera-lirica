  nome: "Là ci darem la mano"

personaggi:
  - nome: Don Giovanni
    voce: "Baritone — the protagonist"
    descrizione: "Spanish nobleman, serial seducer, murderer. No remorse, no fear. His vitality is magnetic and terrifying."
  - nome: Leporello
    voce: "Bass — the servant"
    descrizione: "Loyal to his master out of convenience, he both hates and admires him. He keeps the 'catalogue' of conquests: 2,065 women across Europe."
  - nome: Donna Anna
    voce: "Soprano"
    descrizione: "He attempts to seduce her and kills her father. For her, vengeance becomes a reason to live."
  - nome: The Commendatore
    voce: "Bass — the ghost"
    descrizione: "Donna Anna's father, killed by Don Giovanni. He returns as a stone statue to drag him to hell."
  - nome: Donna Elvira
    voce: "Soprano"
    descrizione: "The abandoned ex-lover. She hates him, loves him, cannot stop loving him. She is the most human character in the opera."
atti:
  - titolo: "The Beginning — A Murder, a Catalogue"
    testo: "Don Giovanni attempts to seduce Donna Anna in her bedroom. Her father confronts him. Don Giovanni kills him in a duel, without hesitation. The servant Leporello shows Donna Elvira — another of Giovanni's victims — the famous 'Catalogue': a thousand and three in Spain alone. Elvira had believed she was special."
  - titolo: "The Middle — A Man Who Never Stops"
    testo: "Don Giovanni seduces, lies, manipulates. He tries to take the peasant girl Zerlina to bed on the very day of her wedding. He constantly escapes those who pursue him. In the cemetery he finds the Commendatore's statue and, out of sheer bravado, invites it to dinner. The statue accepts."
  - titolo: "The End — The Stone Guest"
    testo: "The Commendatore knocks at the door — a statue of ice-cold marble. He seizes Don Giovanni's hand and asks him to repent. Three times he is asked. Three times he says no. The floor opens, flames rise. Don Giovanni is swallowed by hell."
finale: "Don Giovanni is the supreme anti-hero of musical history. Mozart gives us no easy moral: he condemns him, yes, but makes him irresistible to the last. It is the eternal fascination of the forbidden."
