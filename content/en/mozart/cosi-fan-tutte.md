---
title: Così fan tutte
compositore: Mozart
anno: "Vienna, 1790"
tagline: "A cruel experiment on love and fidelity — who comes out unscathed?"
aria:
  nome: "Soave sia il vento"
  descrizione: "A trio of heartbreaking beauty: two women and a cynical old man bid farewell to the departing ships. Mozart knows the scene is a lie — and makes it beautiful anyway."
personaggi:
  - nome: Fiordiligi
    voce: "Soprano"
    descrizione: "Convinced she loves her fiancé with absolute fidelity. Her resistance is the longest — and her fall, the most beautiful."
  - nome: Dorabella
    voce: "Mezzo-soprano"
    descrizione: "Fiordiligi's sister, more frivolous and cheerful. She yields first and with fewer qualms. It is not a flaw — it is honesty."
  - nome: Ferrando
    voce: "Tenor"
    descrizione: "Dorabella's fiancé. He joins the bet convinced he will win. He discovers that love is more complicated than he thought."
  - nome: Guglielmo
    voce: "Baritone"
    descrizione: "Fiordiligi's fiancé. More cynical than Ferrando, he laughs at others' weakness — until it is his turn."
  - nome: Don Alfonso
    voce: "Bass — the cynical philosopher"
    descrizione: "An old philosopher who does not believe in female fidelity. He organises the whole experiment to prove his theory. He is right — and that does not make him likeable."
  - nome: Despina
    voce: "Soprano — the maid"
    descrizione: "Don Alfonso's accomplice. Practical, ironic, without illusions. Her common sense is the comic counterweight to all the sentimental drama."
atti:
  - titolo: "Act I — The Wager"
    testo: "Don Alfonso bets the two young officers that their fiancées — the sisters Fiordiligi and Dorabella — will not remain faithful if put to the test. The plan: the two pretend to leave for the war, then return in disguise as Albanian strangers and court each other's fiancée. Fiordiligi and Dorabella weep desperately at the departure of their loves. Don Alfonso smiles."
  - titolo: "Act I — The Disguises"
    testo: "The two return unrecognisable in moustaches and exotic costumes. They pretend to be struck at first sight by the two women. Dorabella and Fiordiligi are indignant — how dare they? Guglielmo and Ferrando congratulate each other: their women are perfect. Don Alfonso waits."
  - titolo: "Act II — The Fall"
    testo: "Despina persuades the two sisters that a little diversion does no harm. Dorabella yields first, to Guglielmo — her sister's fiancé. Fiordiligi holds out longer, with passionate speeches about fidelity, but eventually she too yields to Ferrando. The two officers are shaken: each has seduced the other's fiancée. Ferrando is wounded. Guglielmo laughs — but bitterly."
  - titolo: "The Finale — The Wedding and the Unmasking"
    testo: "Don Alfonso pushes things to the limit: he arranges a mock wedding between the swapped couples. In the middle of the ceremony, the original fiancés 'return'. They find the evidence of betrayal. Fury, tears, confessions. Then Don Alfonso reveals everything: it was a game. The original couples reunite — perhaps. Mozart does not tell us whether they are truly happy."
finale: "Così fan tutte is Mozart's most modern opera: it punishes no one, absolves no one. It tells us that love is fragile, that fidelity is a difficult choice, and that perhaps Don Alfonso was wrong — not because women are faithful, but because men aren't either."
