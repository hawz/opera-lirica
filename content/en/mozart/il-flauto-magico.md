---
title: The Magic Flute
compositore: Mozart
anno: "Vienna, 1791"
tagline: "A Masonic fairy tale of monsters, magic and the eternal battle between light and darkness"
aria:
  nome: "Der Hölle Rache — The Vengeance Aria"
  descrizione: "The Queen of the Night soars to a high C with supernatural fury. It is the most celebrated and difficult soprano aria in all opera history."
personaggi:
  - nome: Tamino
    voce: "Tenor — the prince"
    descrizione: "A young foreign prince. He sets out to rescue a princess and ends up finding wisdom."
  - nome: Pamina
    voce: "Soprano — the princess"
    descrizione: "Daughter of the Queen of the Night, prisoner of Sarastro. She is no passive victim: she is courageous and risks death for love."
  - nome: Papageno
    voce: "Baritone — the birdcatcher"
    descrizione: "The comic heart of the opera. He wants no wisdom or heroics: he wants to eat, drink and find a wife. The audience adores him."
  - nome: The Queen of the Night
    voce: "Coloratura soprano"
    descrizione: "One of the most iconic characters in all opera. She seems good at first, then reveals herself to be dark and vengeful."
  - nome: Sarastro
    voce: "Bass — the wise man"
    descrizione: "The surface 'villain' who turns out to be the voice of reason and light. An ambiguous and fascinating character."
atti:
  - titolo: "Act I — The Prince and the Mission"
    testo: "Prince Tamino is fleeing a giant serpent when he faints. He is saved by three fairies of the Queen of the Night. The Queen shows him a portrait of her daughter Pamina, kidnapped by the wicked Sarastro, and gives him a magic flute. But when Tamino arrives at Sarastro's palace, he discovers things are not what they seem: Sarastro is wise and benevolent; it is the Queen who is cruel and ambitious."
  - titolo: "Act II — The Trials"
    testo: "Sarastro proposes that Tamino undergo initiation trials to earn Pamina and wisdom. Pamina, believing she is being rejected, is on the verge of taking her own life. The Queen of the Night orders Pamina herself to kill Sarastro with a dagger — Pamina refuses. Papageno, who has absolutely no intention of being a hero, eventually finds his Papagena."
finale: "Tamino and Pamina pass together through the trials of fire and water guided by the flute's music. The Queen of the Night is defeated. Light triumphs. Mozart died two months after the premiere."
